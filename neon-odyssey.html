Project Overview

From static quizzes to a living visual ecosystem

Neon Odyssey is a dual-sided web platform designed to solve a core educational challenge: How do we turn static "True or False" quizzes into highly engaging digital experiences without causing cognitive overload?

Product Strategy & Foundations

Two audiences, one platform

Research & Behavioral Insights

Grounding design in how people actually learn

  • Spatial Learning vs. Text Lists: Research shows learners retain information more effectively when data is anchored to spatial and visual landmarks (e.g., neon signs on buildings) rather than static text blocks.
  • Non-Punitive Feedback Loops: Traditional "X" and "V" marks can feel discouraging. In a gamified context, incorrect choices trigger playful, instant visual consequences encouraging safe trial and error without causing abandonment.

The Game

How the mechanics work

  • Neon Odyssey is an educational content generator game where players are presented with "True or False" questions. The player must collect neon lights from signs that represent the correct answers. Each sign glowing with neon light symbolizes the right response.
  • If the player answers incorrectly, instead of collecting neon lights, the mosquito will encounter a mosquito zapper, getting shocked and losing progress.
  • As the player successfully collects the required amount of neon lights, the mosquito will be able to spread and restore the Northern Lights across the landscapes of America, bringing back their breathtaking glow.

Conceptual Framework & Narrative

A story that makes the mechanics stick

To give the educational mechanics a cohesive visual anchor, the interface uses a light thematic story.

The Story

In the 1950s, the rise of neon lights in America had a devastating impact on the balance of light in the world, threatening the very existence of the Northern Lights.

A mosquito with extraordinary magical powers embarks on a mission to collect neon lights. She will use the energy she gathers to restore the glow of the Northern Lights.
