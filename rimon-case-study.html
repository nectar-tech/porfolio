The Problem

Many users struggle to fully utilize their financial rights and entitlements

There is a significant difficulty in tracking and managing these rights and benefits effectively.

Reasons
  • Lack of awareness regarding benefits
  • Benefits originate from a variety of different sources
  • Difficulty in understanding the details of a benefit
Implications
  • Many rights remain unclaimed
  • Financial loss
  • Waste of time and effort
  • Feelings of confusion and dissatisfaction, or the sense of being taken advantage of leading to a lack of control and security
Interviews

Talking to them

During the research phase, I conducted in-depth user interviews to understand the issue comprehensively. I chose interviews over questionnaires to grasp each person's motivations, needs, challenges, and frustrations better. These interviews included military reservists, victims of hostile acts, and individuals with government recognized disabilities, capturing insights from a broad spectrum of users.

User Research Insights

The issue is not a lack of information, it's an overload of fragmented information and a lack of transparency

Interviewees described feelings of confusion, burden, and a lack of control. Many expressed frustration with the need to log into various websites and track everything manually or in their heads.

What Values and Needs Matter Most

Real-time control and management

Instant visibility into all available benefits and their current status

Saving time and money

Reducing the hours spent navigating bureaucracy and eliminating unclaimed financial loss

Simplicity and transparency

Clear language and a frictionless interface that removes the sense of bureaucratic burden

A sense of satisfaction and utility

Users feel rewarded when they successfully redeem a benefit they didn't know they had

Personalization tailored to interests

Recommendations and notifications scoped to what actually matters to each individual user

Conclusions and Recommendations
  • Consolidation and automated monitoring in a single interface
  • Smart notifications before expiration dates or when new benefits become available
  • A "Total Savings" display
  • An accessible, non-bureaucratic user experience

User Scenarios

A Day with Rimon

Initial Onboarding

Rimon downloads the app and completes a short registration process where he enters his personal details. The app retrieves all the rights he is entitled to and displays his remaining balances. Rimon is surprised to discover rights he was previously unaware of.

Returning to the Product

A week later, Rimon receives a notification: "You have one month left to use your new computer benefit — don't miss out!" He opens the app, reviews the options, and orders a computer through one of the recommended stores. At the end of the month, he receives a summary: "You utilized 4 benefits this month with a total value of 7,500 NIS."

At the Mall

When Rimon arrives at the mall and opens the app, it scans his surroundings and displays a map of all the stores where he can redeem a benefit (chains like Gali, Nine West, To Go, etc.) and marks the discount amount waiting at each store — for example: "In this store, you can redeem a 200 NIS discount on your annual purchase."

Personalized Interests

Rimon selects his interests: "Sports, Culture, Body Treatments." From that moment on, he receives notifications and recommendations for benefits in these areas, such as fully funded tickets to a sports event or mindfulness workshops.

Business Model

White Label for Scale

The app will operate in partnership with official bodies (such as the National Insurance Institute or "Behatzlacha"). This will allow these organizations to increase their benefit utilization rates, while we receive a stable model of licensing and recurring payments.
